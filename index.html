About: I am a researcher and doctoral canditate at the Weizenbaum Institute and the WZB Berlin Social Science Center. At the WZB I am in the research group Politics of Digitalization. At the Weizenbaum Institute I was part of the research group Quantification and Social Regulation and now I am associated with the research group Power, domination and technology.

My PhD thesis investigates computational modeling pracites in algorithmic governance. It is a qualitative study of the processes through epistemic communities create computational models of the social and mobilize them as resources in societal coordination. The thesis is supervised by Jan-Peter Voß and Jan-Hendrik Passoth.

Before starting my PhD I have studied social science and philosophy at Humboldt University, Université Paris 8 and the New School for Social Research.
